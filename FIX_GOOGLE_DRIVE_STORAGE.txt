FIX: Google Drive Service Account Storage Quota Error
=====================================================

PROBLEM:
Service accounts don't have their own storage quota. They can only upload files
to folders that belong to regular Google accounts (like your Gmail account).

SOLUTION:
You need to create a folder in YOUR regular Google account and share it with
the service account.

STEP-BY-STEP FIX:
=================

1. CREATE A FOLDER IN YOUR GOOGLE ACCOUNT
   ---------------------------------------
   - Go to https://drive.google.com
   - Make sure you're logged into YOUR regular Google account (not the service account)
   - Click "New" > "Folder"
   - Name it something like "E-Library Files" or "GDC Library"
   - Create the folder

2. GET THE FOLDER ID
   ------------------
   - Open the folder you just created
   - Look at the URL in your browser
   - The URL will be: https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/FOLDER_ID_HERE
   - Copy the FOLDER_ID_HERE part
   - Example: If URL is https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/1WIA6m9ZqgC0IxTSBOfzmy4WkyfTpWxkF
             Then folder ID is: 1WIA6m9ZqgC0IxTSBOfzmy4WkyfTpWxkF

3. SHARE THE FOLDER WITH SERVICE ACCOUNT
   --------------------------------------
   - Right-click on the folder > "Share"
   - In the "Add people and groups" field, paste your SERVICE ACCOUNT EMAIL
     (This is the GOOGLE_DRIVE_CLIENT_EMAIL value from your .env file)
   - Example: elibrary-drive@your-project-id.iam.gserviceaccount.com
   - Set permission to "Editor" (important!)
   - Click "Send"
   - You don't need to notify the service account, so you can uncheck the notification box

4. UPDATE ENVIRONMENT VARIABLES
   ------------------------------
   - In Render Dashboard, go to your backend service
   - Go to Environment tab
   - Update GOOGLE_DRIVE_FOLDER_ID with the folder ID from step 2
   - Save and redeploy

5. VERIFY SETUP
   -------------
   - The folder should be in YOUR regular Google account's Drive
   - The folder should be shared with the service account email
   - The service account should have "Editor" permission
   - GOOGLE_DRIVE_FOLDER_ID should be set in Render

IMPORTANT NOTES:
================
- The folder MUST be in a regular Google account (your Gmail, etc.), NOT in the
  service account's drive
- The service account MUST have "Editor" permission (not just Viewer)
- The folder ID is the long string of letters and numbers in the folder URL
- After updating the folder ID in Render, you need to redeploy for changes to take effect

TESTING:
========
After fixing, try uploading a file again. The file should appear in the folder
you created in your Google Drive.
